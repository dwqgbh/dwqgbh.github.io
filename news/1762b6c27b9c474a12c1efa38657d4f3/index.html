讲座主题

2025.11.11

Elastic Bound states In the Continuum (BICs): Fundamentals and Applications


讲座摘要

Elastic bound states in the continuum (BICs) are non-radiating vibrational modes embedded in the spectrum of propagating elastic waves, offering an extreme route to trap mechanical energy in open structures [1].

In this seminar, I will briefly introduce the main mechanisms that generate elastic BICs and show how slight symmetry breaking converts them into quasi-BICs with controllable radiation leakage. This tunability makes BICs powerful building blocks for vibration control: by matching radiative and material losses, quasi-BICs can be driven to act as elastic absorbers that strongly absorb and localize vibration energy in compact regions, enabling lightweight vibration absorbers and isolation stages with effective high-Q performance [2]. I will then discuss how the same sharp, interference-driven features can be exploited for sensing, where localized strain fields and steep dispersive responses translate into enhanced sensitivity to added mass, or mechaniocal properties changes [3]. Finally, I will show how coupling BIC-based resonances in non-Hermitian architectures leads to exceptional points (EPs), where multiple eigenmodes and eigenvalues coalesce [4]. Operating near higher-order EPs built from quasi-BICs can, in principle, combine the large energy buildup of BICs with the eigenvalue hypersensitivity of EPs, offering new strategies for ultra-responsive vibration and strain sensors and for switchable vibration absorption states.

嘉宾介绍

Badreddine Assouar received his PhD degree in Materials Physics from Nancy University in France in 2001. In 2002, he became a Research Scientist at “Centre National de la Recherche Scientifique (CNRS)” in France. He obtained his habilitation to supervise research (HDR) in 2007 and became a Research Professor. In 2010, he joined Georgia Institute of Technology in Atlanta as visiting Professor. Since 2013, he created and has led the “Acoustic Metamaterials and Phononics” group at the University of Lorraine, where he is developing research on acoustic and elastic metamaterials, metasurfaces, phononics and SAW devices. He is currently a Director of Research at the CNRS, and since 2021 the Director of the LabCom MOLIERE, a joint industrial-academic research. In the last 15 years, he served on many technical program committees of international conferences and organized numerous international conferences and workshops.. Badreddine was elected a Fellow of the European Academy of Sciences in 2024 and has been a valued member to the APS community as an Associate Editor for Physical Review Applied since 2019.

Prof. Assouar is author or or co-author of more than 170 international peer reviewed publications, in leading international journals including, Phys. Rev. Lett., Nature Review Materials, Advanced Materials, Nature Communications, Science Advances, Phys. Rev. Applied …, and more than 60 invited talks and keynotes over the world.
