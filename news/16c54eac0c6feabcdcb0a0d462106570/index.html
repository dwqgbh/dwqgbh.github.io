2024年进博会希腊文物展宣布仪式

希腊驻上海总领馆 2024-11-11













展览合作宣布仪式现场

2024年11月8日上午，“古希腊之旅 ”（暂定名）展览合作宣布仪式在第七届中国国际进口博览会期间举行。


根据备忘录所提到的规划，该展览将于 2025 年 4 月至 2026 年 3 月分别在三星堆博物馆和良渚博物院展出半年。


本次展览旨在聚焦希腊重要考古遗址，将精选各大遗址出土的重要文物，尤其是金器、青铜雕像等，与三星堆文物形成对话，充分展示双方在文化、艺术、审美等方面的相似性和差异性。

希腊驻上海总领事Georgios Partheniou先生在致辞中表示，此次展览将为中国观众开启一场身临其境的古希腊奇妙文化之旅。这是两国以各自丰富的文化遗产为荣，本着对彼此文化传统的深厚敬意，通力合作的成果。


希腊与中国向来秉持全面求知的理念，将历史文化遗产融入其中。希腊文化部与三星堆博物馆、良渚博物院签署的两份备忘录，实乃文化交流与教育的重要指引。


这些在文化领域的协作，充分彰显了双边关系正稳步迈向增进相互理解、构筑有益理念框架的目标。


On the morning of 8 November 2024, the announcement ceremony of the cooperation of the exhibition ‘The Journey of Ancient Greece’ was held on the sidelines of the 7th China International Import Expo.


The exhibition 'The Journey of Ancient Greece' will be, according to the planning of the memoranda, on display for half a year from April 2025 to March 2026 at the Sanxingdui Museum and the Liangzhu Museum respectively.


The exhibition will intend to bring up the important archaeological sites in Greece, and selects important cultural relics unearthed from the major sites, especially gold and bronze statues, to form a dialogue with Chinese cultural relics, and to demonstrate some of the similarities and differences between the two sides in terms of culture, art and aesthetics. 

The Consul General in his speech, among others, stated: ‘Through these exhibitions the Chinese public will experience an aptly called cultural “Journey to Ancient Greece", a synergy of entities of both countries, which take great pride in their rich heritage and share deep respect for each other’s cultural traditions.


The two signed memoranda between the Greek Ministry of Culture and the Sanxingdui Museum and Liangzhu Museum can truly serve as signposts for cultural exchanges and education as both Greece and China have diachronically pursued knowledge in a comprehensive way, incorporating historical and cultural artifacts. 


These synergies demonstrate that in the cultural field, our bilateral relations are systematically aiming at mutual understanding and a beneficial conceptual framework.’
